# Moral Bias in Data Science: An Analysis of CMSC320 Survey Data
**Author:** Venni Gedipudi  
**University:** University of Maryland, College Park
**Course:** CMSC320 Section(0101)

## Project Overview
Code cleaning and analyzing datasets regarding moral judgment patterns and gender-based double standards within (2023–2026) datasets. The project explores the "Ground Truth" problem in ethics by quantifying how the **student body** evaluates the **jerkiness** of a **protagonist** based on **gender-flipping** scenarios.

## Vocab Key
Jerkiness Score - The numerical rating (0-2) used to measure how "wrong" a character’s actions were, based on student responses.

Gender-Flipping - The method of swapping a character's gender in identical stories to see if the student body changes their judgment based only on sex.

Protagonist - The main person in the doctor, wedding, and trust fund stories whose behavior we are asking the class to judge.

Student Body - The specific group of CMSC320 students who provided the labels (scores) that make up our dataset.

Moral Foundations Theory (MFT) - The idea that our sense of right and wrong comes from different gut feelings, such as a preference for individual freedom versus a respect for tradition.

Individual Autonomy - The freedom to make personal choices without being judged. The data shows students are more likely to give this freedom to men.

Relational Responsibility - The expectation that a person should put others (like family or patients) first. The data shows students expect this more strictly from women.
